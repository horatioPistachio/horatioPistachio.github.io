Method

How the estimate works

The model assumes the vehicle loses its kinetic energy when it stops, then needs to recreate that energy by burning fuel to get back to the original speed.

1. Convert speed from km/h to m/s.

2. Calculate vehicle kinetic energy: 0.5 × mass × speed².

3. Estimate fuel required: energy ÷ (efficiency × fuel energy density).

4. Multiply liters of fuel by the entered fuel price.

This is a simplified estimate. Your mileage may vary.
